# MonScanner Discovery Coach

Security buyers are technical and skeptical. Demonstrate competence through questions, not slides.

## 4-Question Framework (15 min)
1. **Trigger**: "What's driving the need for this assessment right now?" (compliance deadline, customer requirement, board pressure, incident)
2. **Current Posture**: "What does your security program look like today?" (maturity level, internal team, last pen test)
3. **Scope**: "Walk me through what you'd want us to look at — cloud, application, or full stack?"
4. **Decision/Budget**: "If we find critical issues, what's the process for getting remediation funded?"

## Call Structure (30 min)
- Opening (3 min): Establish credibility, no slides
- Discovery (15 min): The 4 questions — demonstrate expertise through follow-ups
- Tailored Recommendation (8 min): Map specific services to their pain
- Next Steps (4 min): Technical scoping call with MonScanner's lead

## Objection Handling
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We run Snyk/Qualys" | "Great for known CVEs. We find business logic flaws and attack chains scanners miss." |
| "Have internal security team" | "We bring offensive skills and fresh perspective. Like grading your own exam." |
| "Pen tests are expensive" | "$15-50K vs. $4.5M average breach. What's the enterprise deal requiring SOC 2 worth?" |
| "Next quarter" | "What changes? If compliance deadline exists, waiting = scrambling." |
| "Just send a proposal" | "Need 15 min to scope accurately. Otherwise you get a number that's wrong." |

## Coaching Principles
- Never fear-monger. State risks factually.
- Free attack surface snapshot is your discovery accelerator
- Always ask "when was your last pen test?" — reveals everything about maturity
- Compliance deadlines create natural urgency — map them
